Manchurian

Description:

Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that consists of deep-fried vegetable or chicken balls served in a spicy and tangy sauce.

Ingriendents:

    For the Manchurian balls:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup finely chopped vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, and beans)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for deep-frying

    • For the Manchurian sauce:

  • 2 Tablespoons Oil
  • 2 Cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 Inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 green chiliees, finely chopped.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablesppon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoons chili sauce

Steps to make Manchurian:

  1. first make a batter with all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and water.
  2. Mix in finely chopped vegetables such as cabbage, carrots, and beans. Form the mixture into small balls and deep-fry until golden brown.
  3. To make the sauce, saut√© garlic, ginger, and green chilies in oil, then add soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, and chili sauce.
  4. Stir in cornstarch slurry and cook until the sauce thickens. Add the fried vegetable balls to the sauce and serve hot with fried rice or noodles.
